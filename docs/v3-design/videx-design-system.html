The Videx
Design System

An editorial system for the show across your stack. Serif display type carries weight, kickers do the labelling, photography breathes. Cards are restrained — one rating, one bookmark, one platform — so the curation is what you read.

v1 · post-polish · April 2026 Maps to src/ module & component inventory

Overview

Videx is the show across your six streaming services. The editorial direction frames it like a weekly magazine: kickers categorise, serif headlines carry the title, italic captions set context, and a cream-on-near-black palette keeps it photographic. The system trades the tile-sea of generic streaming UIs for sectioned editorial rhythm — a hero, a mosaic, a spotlight, then rows.

How to read this doc

Tokens and components are the design contract — anything that uses them inherits the look. The change matrix at the bottom maps every component in components.md and every module in module-map.md to a status: Unchanged UI Update Redesign New. Use it to plan the implementation.

Principles

01
Editorial, not algorithmic
Every section feels chosen. Kickers explain why a row exists. Captions do the work that pure-data UIs leave to the user.
02
Restraint on cards
One rating, one platform, one bookmark. No plan/free pills, no hover labels, no badge clutter. The poster is the hero.
03
Voice in the typography
Fraunces for display: opsz, italics, tight kerning. DM Sans for UI. The serif/sans pairing is the brand.
04
Rhythm beats density
Long pages are deliberate: hero → editorial note → filter chips → mosaic → trending → spotlight → service rows → free → critics → calendar. Variety in component shape, never just a wall of cards.

Voice & tone

Calm, observed, slightly British. Section kickers name the shelf — The Mosaic, The Charts, By Service, Critics' Picks. Captions read like magazine standfirsts: short, italic, present-tense.

EXAMPLE — section caption

"Combined Rotten Tomatoes and IMDb scores. Recent releases only."

EXAMPLE — Editor's Note opening

A quiet week across your six services. Apple TV+ released the film we've been waiting for, BBC iPlayer added three documentaries worth your evening, and a slow-burn Channel 4 drama is the one critics won't stop talking about.

Typography

Two families do all the work. Fraunces (variable, optical-sized) for everything editorial — display headlines, titles on cards, captions, the drop cap. DM Sans for UI — kickers, buttons, meta, navigation.

Variable font usage

Fraunces opsz must be set per role: "opsz" 144 for hero headlines, "opsz" 96 for the drop cap, "opsz" 36 for section titles, "opsz" 18 for body, "opsz" 12 for card titles. The optical sizes are the whole point of the family — without them Fraunces looks generic.

Iconography

Custom 24×24 icon set, line style, currentColor stroke. Stroke width is 1.8 default, 2.4 for active states. Icons used by the system: home · sparkles · search · bookmark · bookmarkF · user · play · info · chev · chevDown · arrow · sliders · star · moon · clock · leaf · flame · bolt · sun · eye · plus · check · heart · download · wand · fingerprint · close. Filled variants are explicit (bookmarkF) — not implied by toggling fill.

Icon contract

Component spec: <Icon name size={20} stroke={1.8} />. Stroke 1.8 reads as line; 2.4 reads as active. Bookmark is the one icon with both line and fill variants — fill means saved.

Magazine Hero (FeaturedHero)

The full-bleed top-of-Home unit. Replaces the previous "auto-rotating carousel of 3-5 cards" with a single editorial cover treatment, swipeable between 3 covers. Backdrop image fills the unit; serif title sits left; service + ratings + a single primary CTA at the bottom.

Image
16:11 backdrop image, full-bleed. Vertical gradient over the bottom 60% so text remains legible regardless of source image.
Type stack
Kicker (DM Sans 10 uppercase, primary). Title (Fraunces ~36 / 600, opsz 96). One-line standfirst (Fraunces italic 14).
Affordances
Primary CTA (filled cream button, 14px radius). Secondary "More info" ghost. Bookmark button top-right matches card bookmark.
Indexing
3 dots bottom-centre, swipe to advance. Auto-advance disabled by default — editorial doesn't auto-rotate.

Bottom nav (BottomNav)

Five tabs, fixed to the viewport bottom, blurred surface. Order is unchanged from the existing app: Home · For You · Browse · Watchlist · Profile. The Watchlist tab carries a count badge.

Surface
rgba(13,13,20,0.85), 20px backdrop blur with 180% saturation. Top border rgba(255,255,255,0.08).
Active state
Icon stroke 2.4 (vs 1.8 inactive). Color is --primary. Bookmark icon swaps to bookmarkF when active.
Badge
Watchlist count, top-right of icon. --primary background, 9px font, suppressed when tab is active.
Position
position: fixed; bottom: 0; with safe-area inset bottom padding. Pages reserve 110px of bottom padding.

Screen — Home

Section order top-to-bottom is the magazine. Variety in component shape is enforced — never two identical rows back-to-back.

  1. Magazine Hero — full-bleed editorial cover, swipeable.
  2. Editor's Note — collapsed strip (tap to expand).
  3. Filter chips — All / Movies / TV / Docs / Anime / UK Free / Under 90 min.
  4. Recently added — kicker "The Mosaic", mosaic row (1 lead + 2 standards).
  5. Trending across your stack — kicker "The Charts", ribbon ranked 1–4.
  6. Editorial spotlight — single full-width title with magazine treatment.
  7. New on each service — three per-service rows with logo + name + scroll row.
  8. Free tonight — small editorial strip on iPlayer / ITVX / C4.
  9. Critics' Picks — wide variant scroll row.
  10. On the calendar — coming-soon strip with date pills.

Screen — For You

Personalised version of the magazine. Greeting header, then mood-driven rooms, then taste-derived rows.

  1. Greeting header — "For Sam · Tuesday evening" + display headline.
  2. Top Pick — single hero card with reasoning.
  3. Taste fingerprint — 6 archetype scores, sparkline.
  4. Mood chip refiner — 6 chips, scrolls horizontally.
  5. Filtered mood mosaic — reflects the active chip.
  6. Featured Mood Room — cover-story treatment, vertical stack on mobile.
  7. 5 more rooms — 2×2 mood-tile grid.
  8. Because you watched X — scroll row with "Why?" action.
  9. From your watchlist — list-style row with poster, meta, Play button.
  10. Outside your usual — 22% designed exploration band, wide variant row.
