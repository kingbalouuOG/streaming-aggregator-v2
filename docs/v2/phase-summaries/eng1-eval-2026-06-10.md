## A. τ (interest-merge threshold) — **decision: keep 0.80**

Pairwise raw cosine over the 16 onboarding clusters' seed centroids (120 pairs):

| Statistic | Value |
|---|---|
| min / median / max | 0.3094 / 0.5598 / **0.7532** |
| Pairs merging at τ=0.75 | 1 (anime-animation × family-kids, 0.7532) |
| Pairs merging at τ=0.80 | **0** |
| Pairs merging at τ=0.85 | 0 |

Top similar pairs: anime×family 0.753, epic-scifi×horror 0.749, dark-thrillers×mind-bending 0.740, feel-good×family 0.717.

**Reading:** at the shipped τ=0.80, similarity-merges never fire on today's taxonomy — the K≤3 cap does all the merging for 4–5-cluster pickers, and the cap merges the closest pair first (which is anime×family when both are picked — the right call). Dropping to τ=0.75 would *always* merge anime×family even for a 2-cluster picker who chose them deliberately as distinct interests. **Keep 0.80**: "merge only near-duplicates" with no current pair qualifying is coherent behaviour, and the cap provides the structural merge pressure.

(Plan §6 said "all 24 onboarding clusters" — the taxonomy has **16**. Plan figure corrected here.)

## B. Synthetic multi-modal profile — **coverage PASS; recall inconclusive (n=2)**

Profile: `feel-good-funny` + `anime-animation` (pair cosine 0.61; the least-similar pair among clusters with ≥6 embedded reps). 17 positive titles, 15 train / 2 held-out. k-means produced K=3, weights [0.467, 0.333, 0.200].

| Check | Result |
|---|---|
| **Coverage** (top-20 of merged pool, distinct source interests) | **3 — PASS** (gate ≥ 2) |
| Baseline contrast (single-centroid top-20, nearest-cluster assignment) | leans 2 |
| Recall@500 single vs multi | 0/2 vs 0/2 — gate (multi ≥ single) technically passes, **not meaningful at n=2** |

**Reading:** the structural fix the brief targets is demonstrated — per-centroid retrieval + weighted interleave guarantees the flagship pool's head spans the user's interests, where the averaged-vector baseline drifts. Recall is bounded by the synthetic profile's size: cluster rep lists yield only 2 held-out titles. The conclusive recall numbers come from **real-profile mode** (`--user-id` + service key) against the prototype accounts and the `taste-profile-tester` permutations once 044 is applied and centroids exist on-device. Do not quote the 0/2 as a result; quote the coverage 3-vs-2.

Avoided title: the merged pool's #1 (`Brooklyn Nine-Nine`, tv-48891) — thumbs-downing your top recommendation is the canonical scenario. Its 10 nearest in-pool neighbours, before: 2 in the top-20.

## Findings filed for close-out

1. **Cluster rep lists are thin — curation, not coverage** *(reclassified 2026-06-10 after a rep-ID audit)*. Initial read blamed embedding coverage; the audit shows 67 of the 68 curated rep IDs are in the catalogue WITH embeddings (sole exception: tmdb 273481, not in `titles`), and catalogue-wide coverage is 98.63% (22,551/22,864 — the remainder is the daily embed cron's normal tail). The real finding: 13/16 clusters only *define* 2–4 representative titles in `tasteClusters.ts`, so their bootstrap seed centroids average very few points. Live behaviour since Phase 3 (the summary vector uses the same lists); ENG-1 makes it per-interest and therefore visible. Fix is a curation task (expand to ~8–10 reps per cluster, Joe-owned, + eval re-run to confirm seed separation) — does not block the phase; EMA + 24h k-means supersede bootstrap seeds within days of real use. → Parking Lot at close-out.
2. **Impressions lack media_type** (plan §1.2 caveat) — `v_training_examples` joins on content_id within session; 0.8% collision class. → Parking Lot at close-out.
3. **Cold HNSW index** blows the statement timeout on first RPC after idle (the documented 5–12s cold start `warmup-foryou` mitigates in-app). The harness warms + retries; PLAT-3's always-warm Worker kills this class.
