# UK streaming market overview

> Stub. Indicative figures from raw primer (April 2026). Refresh from Ofcom Media Nations, BARB SVOD, Enders Analysis, Antenna State of Subscriptions when next reviewed.

## Subscription landscape (April 2026, indicative)

- ~70% of UK households have at least one SVOD.
- Average services per subscribing household: ~2.3.
- Saturation: Netflix and Prime Video at ~50%+ household penetration; Disney+ third; Apple TV+ smaller but growing.
- Public broadcasters (BBC iPlayer, ITVX, Channel 4) covered by TV Licence (BBC) or free ad-supported (ITVX free, Channel 4). High reach, lower per-user time-spent than SVOD leaders.

## Pricing pressure

- Multiple ad-supported tiers launched 2023-2024 (Netflix, Disney+, Prime Video).
- ITVX added Premium tier; remains optional.
- Sky bundles Sky Go with Sky TV subscription (~£26-29/month).
- NOW continues as no-contract Sky alternative.

## Bundling dynamics

- Apple One: Apple TV+ + Music + iCloud + Arcade (£18.95).
- Amazon Prime: TV bundled with shipping benefits.
- Sky Stream / Sky Glass: Sky TV + Netflix bundled in some packages.
- Mobile telco bundles: O2 + Disney+, EE + Prime/Apple TV+/Netflix combos rotate.

## Churn

- Monthly churn rates 4-7% for major SVODs (Antenna data, indicative).
- Pay-cycle churn high: subscribe for one season, cancel after.
- Bundling reduces churn on parent service but can shift to add-on.

## FAST (Free Ad-Supported Streaming TV)

Pluto TV, Samsung TV Plus, ITVX free tier, Channel 4. Ad-funded, no subscription. Less developed in UK than US, but growing.

## Implications for Videx

- Aggregation across paid + free tiers essential; users do not think in service silos.
- "Hidden costs" view (Spend Dashboard) maps to real frustration.
- Coming Soon view differentiates from JustWatch by being personalised to subscribed services.
- Service-spread variety in ranking matters because users actively want to use what they pay for.
