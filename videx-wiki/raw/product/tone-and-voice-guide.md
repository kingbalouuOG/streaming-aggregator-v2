# Tone and Voice Guide

How Videx talks to users in copy. Consistent across onboarding, empty states, error messages, push notifications.

## Voice principles

- **Direct.** Tell users what's happening and why. Avoid hedging.
- **Quietly confident.** No exclamation marks unless the moment genuinely warrants it.
- **Human.** Contractions are fine. Never patronising.
- **British English.** "Personalised", "favourite", "behaviour", "organise". Single quotes for inline references.
- **No hype.** Never "amazing", "incredible", "transform your viewing experience".
- **No emoji.** Visuals do the lifting where colour or cue is needed.

## Examples

### Empty states

| Bad | Good |
|---|---|
| "Oops, no titles found! Try a different search 😊" | "No matches. Try a different keyword or remove a filter." |
| "Looks like your watchlist is empty! Start adding!" | "Nothing in your watchlist yet. Add a title from any detail page." |

### Errors

| Bad | Good |
|---|---|
| "Something went wrong! Please try again." | "Couldn't load this. Check your connection and retry." |
| "Oh no, deep link failed!" | "Couldn't open the app. We'll take you to the website instead." |

### Onboarding

| Bad | Good |
|---|---|
| "Tell us about your amazing taste in shows!" | "Pick a few shows you've watched and liked." |
| "Awesome! Almost done!" | "One more step." |

## Length conventions

- Button labels: 1-3 words. Verb-led where action ("Add to watchlist", "Mark watched").
- Headings: 2-6 words.
- Tooltip / inline help: one sentence.
- Error toasts: one sentence + optional retry CTA.

## Naming conventions

- "For You", not "ForYou" or "for you" in product copy.
- "Home", "Browse", "Watchlist", "Profile" — title case.
- "Mood Room" — title case in copy.
- Service names: use the service's own preferred form (Netflix, Amazon Prime Video, Disney+, NOW, BBC iPlayer, ITVX, Channel 4, Paramount+, Apple TV+, Sky Go).
- "Sign in" / "Sign up" (not "log in" / "register").

## Things to avoid

- Em dashes in user-facing copy. Use commas, colons, or sentence breaks.
- Question marks for non-questions ("Ready to start?" — fine if optional; not as a fake call-to-action).
- "We" in pure-product copy where it implies a team voice. Use it sparingly when transparency genuinely helps ("We use this to learn what you like").
- Jargon: "vector", "embedding", "cluster", "ranker" stay out of user copy. "Recommendations", "matches", "groups" are the user-facing equivalents.

## Push and email

- Subject lines under 40 characters where possible.
- One CTA per message. The CTA verb matches the intent ("Open watchlist", "See what's new on Disney+").
- Cadence: never daily by default. Weekly digest is the cap; opt-in for real-time.
